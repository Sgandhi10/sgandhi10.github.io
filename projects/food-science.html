Food Science NLP

Research Assistant | Data & Machine Learning
NLP Word2Vec Python Data Visualization

Project Overview

Working as a Research Assistant, I applied Natural Language Processing (NLP) techniques to the food science domain. The project aimed to uncover the hidden linguistic and statistical relationships between how humans describe the taste of food and its actual chemical composition.

The Challenge

Sensory descriptions of food are highly subjective, while chemical descriptions are highly objective. Bridging these two datasets is incredibly difficult without human bias. We needed a computational way to map words like "fruity" or "earthy" to specific molecular compounds across massive datasets of food reviews.

The Solution

I developed a machine learning model utilizing Word2Vec to process massive text corpora. By representing words as high-dimensional vectors, the model was able to mathematically identify clusters and correlations between qualitative sensory words and quantitative chemical data.

Technical Implementations

  • Model Architecture: Trained custom Word2Vec models on extensive food science text data to establish mathematical proximity between descriptive adjectives and chemical nouns.
  • Data Visualization: Translated complex, high-dimensional vector data into comprehensible charts and graphs using Orange, Tableau, and Gephi to make the findings accessible to non-technical food scientists.
  • Data Cleaning: Wrote Python scripts to sanitize unstructured review data, removing stop words, applying stemming, and standardizing formatting prior to model ingestion.
