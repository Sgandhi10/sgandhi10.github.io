Project Overview

During my time in high school, I held leadership roles within the TJ Space Program. I acted as both the Sat-4 Lead and the BlueCube Lead, guiding a team of students in the development, power management, and educational outreach for small-scale CubeSats.

The Challenge

With space communication becoming a larger market, the goal for our CubeSat was to test different satellite communication systems in space. This mission utilized two radio systems: a Sat-4 and an Iridium network. The Sat-4 was a proven space communication system; however, the Iridium network was an unproven technology primarily used for oceanic travel communication.

My Role

My work on the team was divided into two main segments: outreach and hardware development. I initially focused on global outreach and education, helping other institutions establish their own CubeSat programs. Later, I transitioned to the Sat-4 Lead, where I managed the electronic communications setup and developed the underlying power and testing infrastructure.

Key Contributions

  • Global Educational Outreach: Connected with international universities to build CubeSat programs and hosted local events to assist high schools in developing smaller CubeSat models to understand aerospace technologies.
  • Sat-4 Communications Lead: Directed the electronic communication setup for the Sat-4 system and provided cross-functional electronics support for other satellite subsystems.
  • Power Systems & Hardware Testing: Designed and built a mock Electronic Power System (EPS) and constructed the FlatSat to facilitate rigorous in-house development and testing procedures.
