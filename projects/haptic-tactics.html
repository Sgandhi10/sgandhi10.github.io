Project Overview

Haptic Tactics aimed to solve a critical training problem in the aerospace sector: drilling. Aerospace assembly requires drilling at a large number of points, many of which are in difficult and inconvenient locations. While traditional training using sample composite materials is effective, it fails to provide the realistic tactile feedback and environmental context that real drilling conditions demand. Haptic Tactics addressed this gap by bringing that feedback into a virtual reality training environment.

The Challenge

Standard XR controllers lack the ability to provide meaningful, variable physical resistance. When a user in a VR simulation drills into composites versus metal, the controller only provides uniform vibration — destroying immersion and severely limiting XR's usefulness for industrial training applications like aerospace.

The Solution

I designed and built a fully custom physical device, 3D printed to closely replicate the form factor of a real drill. It utilized a high-torque motor and a linear actuator to generate active physical resistance, dynamically adjusting in real time based on data received from the VR environment.

Technical Implementations

  • Signal Interception: Authored Python and C scripts capable of intercepting and processing live telemetry directly from the VR headset, translating digital virtual states into actionable hardware commands.
  • Electromechanical Design: Integrated a high-torque motor and a linear actuator to provide variable, real-time pushback against the user's trigger pull, simulating different material densities.
  • Rapid Prototyping: Utilized CAD software and 3D printing to iteratively design the housing and mechanical linkages, ensuring the peripheral was ergonomic and functional alongside standard VR hardware.
