Project Overview

Working within the DREAMs Lab, my research focused on pushing the boundaries of SLA 3D printing by integrating conductive inks. The ultimate goal was to establish a methodology for printing functional printed circuit boards (PCBs) directly inside of structural 3D printed components.

The Challenge

Commercial 3D printing hardware is often heavily locked down by the manufacturer. Our lab possessed legacy SLA machines that physically prevented the use of experimental, third-party conductive inks through hardcoded chip locks and encrypted G-code formatting.

The Solution

To utilize our experimental materials, I had to completely bypass the manufacturer's restrictions. I reverse-engineered the proprietary G-code structure using Python, allowing us to send custom instructions to the hardware and successfully execute prints using our own substrates.

Technical Implementations

  • Reverse Engineering: Analyzed the data packets and standard print files from the legacy hardware to decode the proprietary G-code syntax blocking custom prints.
  • Software Bypasses: Authored custom Python scripts to translate standard CAD slicing instructions into the cracked proprietary format, effectively neutralizing the manufacturer's chip locks.
  • Material Integration: Optimized the piezoelectric jetting and curing processes required to lay down proprietary inks without compromising the structural integrity of the surrounding SLA photopolymer resin.
