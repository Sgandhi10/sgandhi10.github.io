Project Overview

During my time at Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology, I held leadership roles within the TJ Space Program. I acted as both the Sat-4 Lead and the BlueCube Lead, guiding a team of students in the development, power management, and educational outreach for small-scale CubeSats.

The Challenge

CubeSats are severely constrained by size and weight. Our primary engineering challenge was designing a robust electrical power system (EPS) capable of surviving low earth orbit (LEO) while generating enough solar power to run onboard telemetry and research payloads without browning out.

The Solution

I led the design and testing of a complete power distribution network. We managed the circuitry for charging lithium-ion batteries via exterior solar panels and utilized voltage regulators to ensure safe, continuous power delivery across all varying subsystem requirements.

Key Contributions

  • Systems Engineering: Handled the high-level integration of the satellite, ensuring mechanical, electrical, and software teams were operating within strict mass and power budgets.
  • Power Regulation: Engineered the pathways to safely route solar-generated power into battery storage, preventing overcharging while maintaining a stable draw for the onboard computer.
  • BlueCube Outreach: Spearheaded the BlueCube educational initiative, simplifying complex orbital mechanics and aerospace engineering concepts into curriculum taught to younger high school students to spark interest in STEM.
