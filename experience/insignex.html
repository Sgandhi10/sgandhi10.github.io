InSignEx

Developer | Intelligent Agriculture
Embedded Systems Sensors C++ Hardware Design

Project Overview

InSignEx is an embedded hardware project focused on precision agriculture. I developed an intelligent, automated irrigation system capable of monitoring crop health in real-time, allowing for highly efficient water usage and targeted nutrient distribution.

The Challenge

Traditional agriculture relies heavily on timed watering schedules, which leads to massive water waste and suboptimal crop yields. Farmers lack cost-effective, granular data regarding the actual hydration levels and health of individual plant clusters.

The Solution

I engineered a microcontroller-based system that utilized a combination of capacitive soil moisture sensors and precision stem diameter sensors. By continually reading these physical metrics, the system could dynamically adjust watering schedules only when the crop biologically required it.

Technical Implementations

  • Sensor Integration: Calibrated and integrated analog sensors to accurately read environmental variables without succumbing to fast corrosion (a common issue with cheap soil sensors).
  • Logic Control: Wrote embedded C++ to process sensor data streams, applying logic thresholds to trigger relays that controlled water pumps and solenoid valves automatically.
  • Data Visualization: Allowed farmers to track trends over time, helping them understand the correlation between stem growth, water usage, and crop yield.
