Haptic Tactics

Intro to XR | Hardware Lead
Hardware Design Python XR / Unity Bluetooth

Project Overview

Haptic Tactics bridged the gap between digital XR environments and physical tactile feedback. Operating as the Hardware Lead, I developed a custom physical peripheral for the Oculus Quest 2 designed to realistically simulate the physical resistance and vibration of operating a power drill in a virtual space.

The Challenge

Standard XR controllers lack the ability to provide meaningful, variable physical resistance. When a user in a virtual reality simulation drives a drill into a piece of wood versus a piece of metal, the standard controller only provides uniform vibration, destroying the immersion and limiting XR's usefulness for industrial training.

The Solution

I designed and built a custom physical device utilizing a motor and a linear actuator to generate active physical resistance. By intercepting Bluetooth signals from the Oculus environment, the device dynamically adjusted its feedback based on the virtual material being interacted with.

Technical Implementations

  • Signal Interception: Authored Python scripts capable of intercepting and processing live Bluetooth telemetry directly from the Oculus Quest 2, translating digital virtual states into actionable hardware commands.
  • Electromechanical Design: Integrated a high-torque motor and a linear actuator to provide variable, real-time pushback against the user's trigger pull, simulating different material densities.
  • Rapid Prototyping: Utilized CAD software and 3D printing to iteratively design the housing and mechanical linkages, ensuring the peripheral was ergonomic and functional alongside standard VR hardware.
